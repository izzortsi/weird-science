# Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence encompasses the theory and practice of creating computational systems that can perform tasks requiring intelligence. This domain includes concepts related to learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and self-improvement in artificial systems.

## Subdomains

### Self-Improving Systems
Systems that can modify their own behavior, algorithms, or architecture to enhance their performance.

**Concepts:**
- [[godel-machine]] - Universal problem solvers with provably optimal self-modification
- [[self-rewriting-program]] - Programs that can modify their own source code
- [[recursive-self-improvement]] - Iterative enhancement of improvement capabilities
- [[homoiconicity]] - Code-as-data property enabling natural metaprogramming

## Connections to Other Domains

### Systems Theory
Artificial intelligence systems are complex adaptive systems that can be analyzed using the mathematical foundations of systems theory. The behavior of learning algorithms, multi-agent systems, and self-improving programs often exhibits emergent properties characteristic of complex systems.

### Formal Ontologies
AI systems require formal representations of knowledge about the world. Concepts from formal ontologies provide the mathematical and philosophical foundations for knowledge representation, reasoning, and semantic understanding in artificial intelligence.

### Mathematical Foundations
Theoretical AI relies heavily on mathematical concepts from set theory, logic, probability theory, and optimization. These foundations provide the formal tools for analyzing algorithm complexity, learning guarantees, and system behavior.
