# Non-well-founded Hierarchies of Nested Systems

## Overview

A systematic investigation of the definitional foundations of complex systems, proceeding from first principles to articulate a formalism grounded in the recursive nesting of subsystems. The theoretical apparatus permits a rigorous examination of emergence as a structural phenomenon arising from inter-level functional dependencies within hierarchical architectures.

## Line of Investigation

This project develops a formal framework for understanding complex systems through:

1. **System Fundamentals** - Rigorous definitions of systems as valued relations on sets
2. **Subsystem Theory** - Formal characterization of nested structures and part-whole relationships
3. **Hierarchical Organization** - Mathematical treatment of multi-level system architectures
4. **Emergent Properties** - Structural foundations for understanding inter-level dependencies

The framework synthesizes set-theoretic precision with mereological intuitions, characterizing systems by their stratified organization across potentially infinite scales of description.
