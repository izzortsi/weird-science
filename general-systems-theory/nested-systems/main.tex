\begin{abstract}
We undertake a systematic investigation of the definitional foundations of complex systems, proceeding from first principles to articulate a formalism grounded in the recursive nesting of subsystems. The theoretical apparatus thus constructed permits a rigorous examination of emergence as a structural phenomenon arising from inter-level functional dependencies within hierarchical architectures. Our approach synthesizes set-theoretic precision with mereological intuitions, yielding a framework wherein systems are characterized not merely by their constituent elements and relations, but by the stratified organization of subsystems across potentially infinite scales of description.

\keywords{Complex systems \and Emergence \and Hierarchical organization \and Nested structures \and Mereology}
\end{abstract}
