# Foundations of General Systems Theory: A Survey of Formal Definitions

## Overview

A systematic compilation of foundational definitions of systems and major theoretical insights drawn from the mathematical literature on General Systems Theory. This document presents a comprehensive survey of the mathematical formalization of systems theory, tracing conceptual foundations through the seminal contributions of Mesarovic, Takahara, Yi Lin, and other key figures in the formalization of systems theory within set-theoretic and logical frameworks.

## Line of Investigation

This project surveys the mathematical foundations of General Systems Theory by:

1. **Collecting Foundational Definitions** - Systematically compiling rigorous mathematical definitions from seminal GST literature
2. **Tracing Conceptual Evolution** - Following the development of formal systems concepts across multiple authors and frameworks
3. **Establishing Set-Theoretic Foundations** - Grounding system definitions in rigorous set theory and logic
4. **Synthesizing Theoretical Insights** - Identifying common patterns and fundamental principles across different formalizations

## Key Theoretical Insights

The survey reveals several fundamental principles:

1. **Set-theoretic foundations** provide rigorous mathematical substrate
2. **Multiple equivalent formalizations** capture the same intuitions
3. **Hierarchy and nesting** are central organizing principles
4. **Relational perspective** transcends specific realizations

## Historical Context

The formalization of GST emerged through contributions from:
- **Mesarovic & Takahara** - Abstract systems theory
- **Yi Lin** - Set-theoretic approaches
- **Takahashi** - Logical foundations
- **Various authors** - Domain-specific applications
